There is a Challenge or Competition between Boys that who will win the Highest and Maximum Marble in the Kanche (Lakhoti Game). First in this Marble Game (Kanche) boys will make the circle in the sand, clay or road with Chalk or Stick. Then they will keep the Marbles now one by one the marble is targeted. Now the boy with this one marble will be hitting the decided Lakhoti if he hits the marble ball then he will kept the all marbles. By doing focus on the selected Goti he hits it with one closed eye. The marble is to be hold tightly to the forefinger of the left hand of the player. Then stretch the finger back of the forefinger of the right hand to shot the Kanche like arrow. The player has to strike out the Marble Ball to push it from its place. If the first player wins then next player takes his turn with the left over marbles. There is a one more method to play the Marble Game (Kancha). In a circle dug a small hole and throw the Lakhoti in the hole to win. The player who succeed to get more number of marbles wins the Kancha (Goli) Game. Goti (Marbles) for Childrens like Toy game. The beginning of spring and the beginning of autumn are considered to be important junctions of climatic and solar influences. These two periods are taken as sacred opportunities for the worship of the Divine Mother Durga. The dates of the festival are determined according to the lunar calendar. Each woman wears traditional three coloured dress during Navaratri. Navaratri represents a celebration of the Goddess Amba, (the Power). Navaratri or Navadurga Parva happens to be the most auspicious and unique period of devotional sadhanas and worship of Shakti (the sublime, ultimate, absolute creative energy) of the Divine conceptualized as the Mother Goddess-Durga. A whole chapter in the tenth mandal of the Rigveda addresses the devotional sadhanas of Shakti. The "Devi Sukta" and "Isha Sukta" of the Rigveda and "Ratri Sukta" of the Samveda similarly sing paeans of praise of sadhanas of Shakti. In fact, before the beginning of the legendary war between the Pandavas and Kauravas in the Mahabharata – a foundational Sanskrit epic in the Hindu tradition – Lord Krishna worshipped Durga, the Goddess of Shakti, for the victory of the Pandvas.
